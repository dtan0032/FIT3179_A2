Global Suicide Rates (1985 to 2016)

Introduction

According to the World Health Organisation, for every suicide there are many more people who attempt suicide every year. As we will discover, suicide rates have stabalised but it still is relatively high compared to the past. We will be investigating the characteristics of the suicides from 1985 to 2016. These characteristics we will be investigating are range from a combination of countries, sex, age group and Human Development Index.

Global Suicides/100K Population

Here, the line graph shows overall trend in global suicide rates have increased since 1985.

From 1985, it was increasing quickly and peaked in 1995 with a staggering suicide rate of 14660 suicides per 100k population. To put that into perspective, it was that 1 suicide in every 15 person born back in 1985 but increased to 1 suicide for every 7 person born in 1995.

It then slowly decreases to 8254 in 2015 which is roughly 1 suicide in 13 people born.
